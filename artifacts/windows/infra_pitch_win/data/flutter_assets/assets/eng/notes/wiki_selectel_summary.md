# Selectel Overview (Wikipedia / iKS-Consulting)

> **Scrape context:** Wikipedia + market reports consolidated to keep a clean profile for reference links inside reports.

- **Profile:** Russian cloud and data center provider founded in 2008 by Vyacheslav Mirilashvili and Lev Leviev (ex-VK investors). Operates six DCs across Moscow, St. Petersburg, and Leningrad region with a seventh “Yurlovsky” site under construction (announced May 2022). Converted to a joint-stock company in July 2024.
- **Market share:** iKS-Consulting (2023) ranks Selectel #3 in RU cloud infrastructure with ~8.5% share; leads bare-metal rental at ~56%.
- **History milestones:** Built Tsvetochnaya (1 & 2), Berzarina, and Dubrovka campuses between 2008–2015; became a K-root host in 2015; partnered with SONM on blockchain pilots in 2017; entered Novosibirsk (2019) and Tashkent (2020).
- **Products:** Dedicated and virtual servers, colocation, Virtual Private Cloud (2015), VMware-based public cloud (2018), managed DB/object storage, Managed Kubernetes, on-prem private cloud installs (2023), and a Debian-based enterprise OS (2024).
- **Recognition:** Multiple National Data Center Awards (2019–2024) in “Cloud of the Year” and “Data Center of the Year” categories.
- **Reputation watch-outs:** Security researchers note selective takedown policies, so marketplace governance must anticipate scrutiny.
